## On Writing About Writing

[I've been here before](./start).

I didn't really express _why_ to write in that unfinished post from a couple of years ago, I still talked about everything around writing. I talked about building websites, buying domains, plans, Digital Gardens, etc.

My thoughts are a lot more clear now. Writing helps me think. Writing forces me to think. Right now in my field (software development), we have AI writing more and more of the things we used to have to expend thinking energy on: code, explanations of code, PRDs, documentation, etc. The thinking has shifted. The cost of knowledge, or information is at an all-time low, so thinking is more important than ever.

Thinking helps me do my job. It helps me figure out slop from not. It helps me communicate to both carbon and silicon. It helps me decide to set a timer for 25 minutes so I don't just end up writing for ever (4:34 left). When a pattern becomes useful, I can abstract it into an interface habit, so I don't have to think an can just do.

Writing requires thinking, just about always. Turning off the autocomplete and trying to put thoughts into language is, as best as I can tell, a uniquely human experience. LLMs run sophisticated algorithms to generate the next most acceptable token. Maybe that's how human consciousness works, I'm pretty doubtful. Either way, writing does a lot more for me, my humanity, my thinking, my soul than receiving tokens from an LLM.

So I'm going to start writing more (again).

Last time I went through this phase, I just posted my writings in [markdown on GitHub](https://github.com/brookslybrand/brookslybrand.com/blob/main/content/getting-tuberculosis.md). This worked well, I was writing! I figured I'd pick up the blog piece eventually, but I didn't and I stopped writing.
