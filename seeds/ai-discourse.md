tl;dr: it kind of sucks

so many "YNGMI if you don't try this new technique I just learned about yesterday". It's fear mongering. It's FUD. It's not helpful.

On the other side people are scared this thing will take their jobs, and seem lost as how to adapt. There's a lack of curiosity.

With Remix, we (Ryan especially) are building much of it with AI, and testing the API against AI's ability to use it. Many wince at this, thinking this is letting the machine tell us what to do. I think it betrays a pretty fundamental misunderstanding of what LLMs are. They are statistics. They are aggregations of all developer (and so likely the most average-est of developers). If they trip over an API, many developer will too. If they don't, many developers won't either.

---

another angle

I am feeling mildly depressed about AI and about my work.

Not so much in the way I've seen others, where they are fearful and sad that LLMs can solve coding problems much quicker (and sometimes better) than they can. Though, I am sad other people are sad about this.

I'm depressed because everything feels useless, and it's hard to know what to spend effort on.

Did you complete a task? Could an LLM have done it faster? Well you wasted your time then.

Are you using ClawdBot? No?! You're wasting your time, leaving time on the table, leaving alpha on the table.

It's just all so annoying, and basically no one knows what they're talking about.

It's incredibly what can be built in a day, but there's also a lot built that doesn't really need to be built. If you're learning, trying something new, stretching your skills with these tools, that's great! But why does your good time mean other people are falling behind, and why is that your problem?

Tech is a disruptive, "move fast and break things" industry, even after we've seen how much and how many we can break. I don't think it'll be different, and many ways I think it just attracts those types of people. I don't want to ask the water to be different, I just want to learn how to swim in it without getting caught up in the currents that lead nowhere

3 body problem: when all scientific discovery stopped. It stopped because experiments could not be duplicated, so results could not be verified and nothing could be learned.

Could we have the opposite problem. We only rely on machines that extract from and replicate what they've already seen. We stop coming up with new results because we don't try anything new, we just rely on what's already been done.
